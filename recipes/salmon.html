Baked Salmon Steaks

"Salmon that melts in your mouth..."

"Pan-fried salmon steak is a delicacy when seasoned with sage and cooked in butter. It's a delicious and seemingly fancy restaurant-level dish that is in fact very easy to prepare, making it the ideal choice for a weeknight dinner."

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon steaks, skin on, bones removed
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 2 lemons

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 200C.
  2. Squeeze the lemons into a dish large enough to fit both salmon steaks, then add the soy sauce. Place salmon in prepared dish. Turn it over once to ensure an even coating of the marinade.
  3. Place each salmon steak into a separate tin foil sheet and create a rectangular shape around the base of the salmon with the foil. The purpose of this step is to ensure that a large amount of the marinade can be drizzled over the salmon steak so it has a 'poaching effect' when baking. This ensures that the meat is tender whilst still being cooked through.
  4. Put enough marinade inside the foil with the salmon steak to cover at least half a centimetre of the base. Then finish wrapping the tin foil over the top of each steak so they are both completely covered.
  5. Place the tin foil-covered steaks onto an oven tray and bake for approximately 25 minutes.
  6. Take the salmon steaks out of the tin foil and serve with or without a dash of the marinade remaining inside the foil.
